PHOTOGRAPHY

Photography is a captivating art form that has been with us since the 1800s. It’s a visual medium that captures moments, emotions, and stories through the lens of a camera.

Photography Defined:

Photography can be broken down into its Greek origins: “photo” (meaning light) and “graphy” (meaning drawing). Essentially, photography is the process of capturing light to create an image. Whether you’re using a film camera or a digital one, the magic lies in how light interacts with the medium.

THE EXPOSURE TRIANGLE

The exposure triangle is a fundamental concept in photography. It involves three critical aspects that work together to determine the exposure of an image

Three critical components:

1. Aperture: This is the opening in the lens through which light passes. When you press the shutter button, the aperture adjusts to allow a specific amount of light in.

2. ISO: After passing through the aperture, light lands on either a digital sensor (in digital photography) or light-sensitive film stock (in film photography). ISO measures the sensor’s or film stock’s sensitivity to light.

3. Shutter Speed: The camera’s shutter opens for a specific duration, exposing the image to light. Shutter speed is measured in milliseconds to minutes.

PHOTOGRAPHY'S PURPOSE

1. Creating Art: Photographers express their creativity, emotions, and perspectives through their images.

2. Documenting Events: From weddings to historical moments, photography preserves memories and stories.

3. Producing Commercial Work: Advertising, fashion, and product photography fall into this category.

FILM vs. DIGITAL PHOTOGRAPHY

Film Photography: It’s nostalgic, with a tactile process involving chemical development. Film enthusiasts appreciate its unique qualities, grain, and unpredictability.

Digital Photography: Instant feedback, post-processing, and convenience define digital photography. It’s accessible to beginners and professionals alike.
